## Verdict: CONDITIONAL GO — but not on the thesis as previously framed

Demand-side fundamentals **improved** since the June 2026 baseline, and the honest anti-overlay positioning is now validated by the DOJ, the FTC, and litigation data simultaneously. But the "$249/mo agency **white-label scanner**" framing is **dead on arrival** — white-label scanning is already given away free by accessiBe and sold as a category by several vendors. What remains verifiably unoccupied is narrower and better: **the white-labeled, dated+hashed evidence RECORD** anchored against $1,250–2,750 manual audits, sold by hand to agencies whose clients face repeat-litigation exposure.

## The 8 verified findings

### 1. Title II urgency formally deferred to 2027/28 — confirmed at the source
DOJ Interim Final Rule, published **April 20, 2026** (Federal Register 2026-07663, effective immediately): entities ≥50k population move to **April 26, 2027**; smaller entities/special districts to **April 26, 2028** (28 CFR 35.200(b)). Only the WCAG 2.1 AA *technical* deadlines moved — general nondiscrimination duties remain enforceable, private plaintiffs can still sue, and an NFB lawsuit challenges the delay. Comment period closed June 22, 2026 with the rule intact. *(3-0 ×2, HIGH)*
- https://www.federalregister.gov/documents/2026/04/20/2026-07663/extension-of-compliance-dates-for-nondiscrimination-on-the-basis-of-disability-accessibility-of-web

### 2. CORRECTION: the US lawsuit market GREW — and repeat litigation is structural
**5,114 total** digital-accessibility suits in 2025 (record; 3,117 federal, **+27% vs 2024's 2,452**), cross-verified UsableNet × Seyfarth (independent defense-side tracker, within ~2.5%). **1,427 defendants had been sued before; 46% of federal cases are repeat defendants** (40%+ in 2024 too — structural). This replaces the June "flat/cyclical" read. Nuance: 2024 dipped, so part of 2025 is rebound. Caveat: re-litigation proves *exposure*, not proven willingness to pay for an evidence ledger. *(3-0 ×2, HIGH)*
- https://blog.usablenet.com/ada-web-lawsuit-trends-2026 · https://www.adatitleiii.com

### 3. Honest anti-overlay positioning validated from three independent directions
(a) DOJ in the April 2026 IFR: advanced tech "such as generative AI, does not **yet** reliably automate the remediation of inaccessible content at scale". (b) No meaningful reduction in suits against widget users (~95–155/month through 2025; ~22.6% of H1-2025 suits hit overlay-equipped sites). (c) FTC's $1M accessiBe fine (Jan 2025). Double-edge: DOJ made the AI statement to justify *delaying* enforcement. *(3-0 ×2, HIGH)*

### 4. The $249/mo white-label SCANNER is undercut — accessiBe gives one away FREE
accessiBe's partner program (explicitly targeting digital agencies) includes a **free white-labeled accessScan** (brandable iframe, custom logo/colors) as lead-gen, plus white-label widget UI and marketing material, no joining fee. **Scope limit that preserves the seam:** what they white-label is a lead-gen scan funnel + overlay UI — NOT a multi-framework, dated+hashed evidence-record deliverable. *(3-0 ×3, HIGH)*
- https://accessibe.com/partners

### 5. White-label agency accessibility tooling is an established category — with zero published pricing
AccessibilityChecker.org sells "fully white-label and brand the platform as your own" to agencies (similar programs: achecks.org, allaccessible.org, testparty.ai, accessibility.eu, allyable.com). Level Access runs a tiered Verified/Certified/Expert agency partner program (expanded March 2026). **No vendor publishes partner economics** — no prices, revenue share, or commissions anywhere. Notable: AccessibilityChecker.org's list price tops out at **$249/domain/month** — the exact planned AccessLens price point, but branded and per-domain. Implication: $249/mo has no public comp; it can only be validated in discovery calls. *(3-0 ×3, HIGH)*
- https://www.accessibilitychecker.org/businesses/agency/ · https://www.levelaccess.com/partner-with-level-access/

### 6. AudioEye proves the agency channel works at scale — and explicitly refuses white-label
~$41.2M ARR (Q1 2026), **~59% of ARR through the Partner & Marketplace channel** (~$24M/yr, SEC-reportable), thousands of partners (Hibu, Dealer.com, GoSite, Simpleview, CivicPlus). Verbatim FAQ: "We do not allow white-boxing/white-labeling of our brand or technology." Caveat: named partners are mostly vertical platforms/CMS vendors, not boutique agencies — strong evidence for *reseller* demand, indirect for *white-label* demand. *(3-0 ×3, HIGH)*
- https://www.audioeye.com/partners/agency/

### 7. Typical agency partner economics are THIN
accessiBe: $350K/month commissions across 5,000+ partners → **~$70/month average per partner** (their own blog elsewhere says $350K *annually*, which would be ~$70/yr). Commission = 20% of first 12 months on ~$490/yr plans (~$98/yr per referred client); their own featured case study cites a 3.3% MRR lift. Reselling is side revenue, not a profit center → agencies won't pay $249/mo for tooling **unless it attaches to a deliverable they bill for** (the audit/evidence record). *(2-1, MEDIUM — the one split vote)*

### 8. Fallback wedges: only PDF has a hard anchor, and it's collapsing
Manual PDF remediation quoted ~**$5/page**; Ohio State estimated **~$20M** for its 110k+ noncompliant PDFs — then switched to an AI solution at **~$0.06/page (~80× cheaper)**. The wedge is a race against commoditization, and its higher-ed urgency was tied to the now-delayed Title II deadline. **No evidence survived verification** for EU-procurement, VPAT-automation, or consultant-enablement wedges, and both cold-email benchmark claims were refuted — those remain unproven, not disproven. *(3-0, MEDIUM)*
- https://www.insidehighered.com/news/government/colleges-localities/2026/01/21/higher-ed-prepares-new-era-ada

## Where the first dollar most plausibly comes from (ranked)

1. **Hand-sold agency white-label EVIDENCE RECORD** (not scanner) — pitch: "your client was sued once; 46% of federal defendants get sued again; here is the dated, hashed, 16-framework record you deliver under your brand, anchored against the $1,250–2,750 audit you already sell." No incumbent ships this artifact white-labeled; AudioEye refuses white-label outright; the free accessiBe scanner is a funnel, not a deliverable.
2. **Same artifact sold to repeat-defendant SMBs directly** (via the lawsuit-record public data) — weaker: no channel leverage, considered purchase.
3. **PDF remediation adjacency** — only if an agency asks; anchor real but collapsing 80×; deadline driver deferred.
4. EU-procurement / VPAT automation / consultant enablement — no verified evidence; treat as unproven.

## What this changes about the validation sprint

Nothing about *doing* it — everything about the *pitch*. Drop "white-label scanner" language entirely (accessiBe gives that away). Lead with the evidence record as a billable deliverable inside the agency's existing audit line item. The repeat-defendant statistic (46%) is the single strongest verified demand hook found in three research passes. The $249 price point has zero public comps — it is a discovery-call question, not a landing-page fact.
